Path Sorting Overview

In the first phase, path sorting estimates the cost (length and diffraction) of each potential reflection path based on the results of the ray tracing. The paths are then processed in ascending order until a maximum of n (the user-specified number of reflection paths) paths are validated. To reduce flickering (switching between paths), the previous paths are also validated. In the second phase, the best n paths are selected from the current and previous validated paths based on their final real costs.
