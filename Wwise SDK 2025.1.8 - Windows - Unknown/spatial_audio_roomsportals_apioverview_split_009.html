Transmission of Room Tones and a Room's Diffuse Field

In addition to sound transmission of individual emitters within a Room, Wwise Acoustics models sound transmission of the Room itself, including the contribution of the diffuse reverb field, as well as any Room tones that are playing inside a Room, transmitting through walls.

Transmission loss filtering and attenuation is applied at the input of a Room Auxiliary Bus, so that individual emitters can contribute differently to the reverb. However, it is the output of the Room Game Object that is spatialized. It is necessary to set AkRoomParams::transmissionLoss to properly spatialize the Room's reverb. A low transmission loss value emphasizes panning the Room towards the Room's center (the center of the Room bounding box) and a high value emphasizes panning towards the Room's Portals.

Because Room tones do not have a point source position, they also rely on AkRoomParams::transmissionLoss to simulate transmission. Therefore, it is always necessary to have a representative value assigned to the Room's AkRoomParams::transmissionLoss.
